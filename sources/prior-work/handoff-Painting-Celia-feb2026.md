## 7. Decisions Made and Why

**Round format for the middle painting** — Decision: 20-inch round wooden panel. Alternatives considered: rectangular canvas. Reasoning: the round format has no corners, which resonates with the theme of having nowhere to hide; it also enables rotation as a performative element, which a rectangle couldn't do as elegantly.

**Rotation as performance** — Decision: the middle painting will be rotated three times during the reading (dark at top → level → light rising). This transforms a static illustration into a kinetic participant. Reasoning: Maya wanted the visual to mirror the emotional journey in real time, not just sit beside it. This was a breakthrough moment in the conversation — she built on Claude's suggestion of "dynamic elements" and specified the exact mechanic herself.

**Using leftover magenta paint** — Practical decision with thematic resonance. Magenta as the color of the middle painting fits the transitional emotional tone (neither the shadow of the first painting nor the light of the third). Practical resource management aligned with artistic intent.

**Companion piece as separate project** — Maya framed the deep-dive analysis explicitly as feeding a *companion* writing project, not as revision or sequel. This distinction matters. The companion piece builds on *Painting Celia* but has its own identity.
